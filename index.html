About Me

I am Ashutosh Sharma, a Ph.D. candidate in Biochemistry at IIT Delhi, working on the role of small non-coding RNAs in Mycobacterium tuberculosis, particularly in antimicrobial resistance and stress adaptation.

In addition to my academic work, I am the creator and host of PhDMonk, a YouTube podcast that shares stories and insights from the world of research, highlighting curiosity-driven learning and the pursuit of scientific growth.

My research experience spans premier Indian institutes like IIT Delhi, IISER Bhopal, and AIIMS, with core strengths in RNA biology, molecular cloning, protein biophysics, and structural biology. I’ve also been involved in mentoring, teaching, and independent research leadership.

Research Interests

  • sRNA-mediated regulation in M. tuberculosis: Exploring how bacterial sRNAs (e.g., MTS1338) influence gene expression in stress and drug-exposed conditions.
  • Mechanisms of antimicrobial resistance: Investigating the molecular mechanisms underlying bacterial survival and adaptation under antibiotic pressure.
  • Protein biophysics & structural biology: Studying the structure and function of proteins using FPLC, CD spectroscopy, DSC, TSA, and other analytical techniques.
  • Synthetic nanobody screening & engineering: Designing nanobody tools via phage and ribosome display for targeting key biomolecular systems.

Outreach & Leadership

Creator & Host, PhDMonk Podcast — A science communication initiative on YouTube focused on the life, lessons, and mindset of researchers.

Research Contributions

  1. PhD Research (2023–Present) – IIT Delhi: Small RNA regulation in M. tuberculosis under drug and stress; RNA-protein interaction assays and sRNA pathway dissection.
  2. Senior Research Fellow, IIT Delhi (2023–2024): Focused on RNA-RNA, RNA-protein, and DNA-protein interactions and post-transcriptional regulation.
  3. Junior Research Fellow, IISER Bhopal (2019–2022): Structural and biophysical work on E3 ligases and ubiquitin; nanobody design and protein characterization.
  4. Master’s Thesis, AIIMS New Delhi (2019): Cloning, expression, purification, and biophysical characterization of MurD protein from A. baumannii.
